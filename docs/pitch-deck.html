Risk in rural India is covariant — we model it that way

  • Maize spikes → every poultry unit in the district is hit (feed ≈ 65% of cost), dairy partially (fodder ≈ 35%), food processing untouched
  • Existing tools score borrowers one at a time — they can't see a cluster failing together
  • Sanket writes the cluster bulletin a DDM or bank regional office actually needs

Auto-generated during the demo you just saw

"Poultry cluster, Wardha: maize price spike (z=4.6). 9 units exposed (~65% cost sensitivity), 3 showing combined stress. Recommended: advance input-purchase advisories; review EMI schedules."

This is the deepest technical differentiation — covariant risk is THE actuarial reality of agri-finance, and per-borrower scorers miss it. The bulletin text on screen is real system output.

No black boxes — enforced in the schema, not the slide
